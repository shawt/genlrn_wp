“When am I going to use this in the real-world?”

In a time of increased testing and Common-Core Standards, it can be tempting to focus exclusively on content, ignoring the context and the motivation and interests of the student. This results in a superficial type of learning. Students may do well on a test, but often they will lack the deeper understanding needed to apply that knowledge beyond the class or the test. Learning is temporary, often vanishing shortly after the school year or the test is finished. Students begin to view school as irrelevant to their personal goals. This damages the climate and culture of a school as a community of learners.

STEM Based Learning

By forging connections across the disciplines of Science, Math, Engineering, and Technology, STEM schools help students see the relevance and real-world applications of their learning. Great STEM schools design engaging and motivating projects that demand a high level of mastery of content in order to solve complex, authentic problems that drive student interest and passion. Using a project-based approach adds relevance and authenticity to what students learn. STEM in schools produces learning outcomes that stick. In STEM schools, students can apply math, science and engineering skills to research real problems. They can apply a design thinking process to invent solutions to those problems. Whether it is inventing an electronic device to capture biomedical data or creating a method for producing sustainable energy, the learning that students do through STEM based learning will stay with them for a lifetime.
